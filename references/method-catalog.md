# Method Catalog

Use this catalog when `x-router` needs a complete view or when deciding whether to promote a subsection into a standalone skill.

## x-purpose

- Be useful: ask who benefits and how reality improves.
- Fight for the future: prefer work that creates a future worth being excited about.
- Obsess for success: sustained focus is easier when the work matters.
- Start before ready: if the thing should obviously exist, early resistance is not decisive.
- Create more than consume: favor real value creation over extraction.
- Work like hell: reserve intensity for work important enough to justify it.
- Feel fear directly: inspect fear, then decide from evidence.

## x-thinking

- Truth calibration: make reality more important than ego.
- First principles: reduce to facts and hard constraints.
- Thinking in limits: test the idea at extreme scale or edge cases.
- Less wrong: update when evidence changes.
- Direct source: go as close to the work/data/problem as possible.

## x-engineering

- Engineering creates value: valuable ideas must become working systems.
- Engineering is magic: creating new useful things is the practical miracle.
- Engineering wins: technology changes competitive reality.
- Chief engineer mindset: understand product, cost, design, manufacturing, and operations.
- Good taste: notice simplicity, fit, beauty, and product feel.

## x-5-step-algo

- Question requirements: every requirement needs owner, reason, evidence, and current validity.
- Delete: remove parts, processes, features, meetings, settings, code paths, and approvals.
- Simplify: optimize only what survives deletion.
- Accelerate: shorten cycle time after direction is validated.
- Automate: automate last.
- Overdelete/addback: define when a deletion went too far and what evidence restores it.

## x-teams

- Group with a goal: a company is people gathered to make products or services.
- Exceptional ability: critical work requires exceptional owners.
- Builder culture: reward making, shipping, and reality contact.
- Special forces: keep critical teams lean and high ownership.
- Feedback over feelings: make bad news visible quickly.

## x-org

- Remove boundaries: let people talk across org lines for the good of the work.
- Simple communication: use clear language and direct ownership.
- Innovation needs permission to fail: non-catastrophic failure is a learning input.
- Meetings and approvals must justify themselves.
- Legacy rule expiry: old process should expire unless rejustified.

## x-urgency

- Do not waste time: time is the scarce resource.
- Speed is offense and defense.
- Do things in parallel when dependencies allow.
- Break down the impossible into resources, constraints, and first proof.
- Set aggressive timelines with explicit uncertainty.
- Use one key scoreboard metric.

## x-manufacturing

- We must make stuff: production is the proof.
- The real work: ideas do not matter unless made real.
- Factory as product: the production system is part of the product.
- Attack the constraint: one bottleneck sets the rate.
- Supplier constraint: dependency weakness can become system weakness.
- Manufacturing moat: production quality, cost, and speed compound.

## x-founder

- All-in conviction: big bets need mission-level justification.
- Adversity: pain can reveal or build capacity.
- Eat glass: work on the problem the company needs, not the enjoyable one.
- Courage under uncertainty: important work can be rational despite low odds.
- Runway: survival constraints must be explicit.

## x-company-building

- Prototype proof: build proof before polish.
- Listen and correct fast.
- Sequenced strategy: beachhead, proof, scale, mission expansion.
- Survival runway: keep the company alive long enough to learn.
- Public reality: do not confuse perception with product truth.
- More for less: create more user value at lower cost or friction.
- Anchor trust: earn early institutional/customer trust through proof.

## x-future

- Future worth building: work should increase possibility.
- Abundance: ask what becomes cheaper, safer, faster, or widely available.
- AI and robotics: inspect automation leverage and transition risks.
- Sustainable energy: scale must be energy-aware.
- Human-machine upgrade: improve human capability, not only machine output.
- Autonomy transition: plan the move from manual to autonomous systems.

## x-risk

- AI risk: capability without alignment or control can create systemic downside.
- Regulation drag: rules accumulate and can freeze progress.
- War: civilization is more fragile than it feels.
- Energy: unsustainable energy can become a binding constraint.
- Population: demographic collapse changes long-horizon systems.
- Asteroids and rare shocks: low-frequency risk still needs resilience.

## x-multiplanetary

- Civilization backup: reduce dependence on one fragile base.
- Gateway milestone: identify the first enabling step that unlocks the rest.
- Mission metric: define the equivalent of mass to Mars.
- Capability thresholds: stage impossible work into thresholds.
- Build the new world: after the gateway, plan durable infrastructure.

## x-reading

- Fiction: imagination, philosophy, society, futures.
- Science: reality, life, universe, climate, long-termism.
- Rocket science and engineering: rockets, structures, propulsion, manufacturing.
- History: war, civilizations, leaders, mistakes.
- AI and machine learning: capability, alignment, control, deep learning.
- Business and economics: capitalism, startups, wealth creation, strategy.
