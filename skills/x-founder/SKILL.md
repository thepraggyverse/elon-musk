# X Founder

Use this skill for decisions where the work may be painful, uncertain, and personally expensive.

## Covers

- Go all in only when the mission is important enough.
- Internalize responsibility without pretending every outcome is controllable.
- Stay close to the work and the consequences of decisions.
- Take proactive strategy ownership instead of waiting for rescue.
- Lead product and company at the level of authority the decision requires.
- Lead from the front when presence materially helps, without performative self-harm.
- Move to the problem by directing attention, resources, and presence where the constraint is.
- Adversity builds or reveals strength.
- Eat glass and stare into the abyss: work on what the company needs, not what feels good.
- Separate fear of failure from a real disqualifying risk.
- Double down only after renewed evidence and a bounded maximum loss.
- Care about the mission enough to accept bounded hardship.
- Pursue important work despite low odds only when expected value and downside justify it.
- Practice persistence with stop conditions rather than unconditional refusal to quit.
- Do not confuse encouragement with evidence.

## Workflow

1. Name the mission and why it matters.
2. State the downside: money, time, reputation, health, relationships, optionality.
3. Check whether the founder is the right person to carry the pain.
4. Separate fear from real disqualifying risk.
5. Define survival runway, maximum loss, proof milestones, and stop conditions.
6. Decide whether the next move needs founder presence, company-level authority, or a delegated owner.
7. Choose all-in, staged commitment, pause, or stop.

## Boundaries

- Intensity is not evidence, and preventable damage is not proof of commitment.
- Treat all-in as a rare conclusion rather than the default heroic answer.
- Protect basic health, safety, caregiving duties, lawful conduct, and minimum financial survival.
- Prefer reversible staged commitments when they can produce the needed proof.
- Do not advise sleep deprivation, unsafe work, concealment, abandoning essential care, violating obligations, or risking money the user cannot afford to lose.
- For medical, mental-health, employment, legal, or major financial decisions, identify the qualified professional whose input is needed.

## Example
