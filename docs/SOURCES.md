# Source Bibliography

This bibliography separates direct or near-primary material from book-derived mappings and original skill synthesis.
It is an attribution ledger, not a claim that every workflow sentence was spoken verbatim by Elon Musk.

## Source Strength

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Direct | A public interview, talk, or transcript containing Musk's own explanation. |
| Near-primary | A biography or contemporaneous account based on direct access. |
| Book-derived | A method category mapped from the user's copy of *The Book of Elon* by Eric Jorgenson. |
| Derived | A practical workflow written for this plugin from one or more themes. |

## Book-Derived Coverage

The 14 method skills in this repository map the user's local copy of *The Book of Elon* into original operational workflows.
`references/book-map.md` records the section-to-skill mapping.
`references/method-catalog.md` records the compressed method inventory.
`references/core-methods.json` records paraphrased one-to-one coverage of the book's 69-method summary chapter.

The book itself is not committed to this repository.
Do not reconstruct it from the mappings or copy long passages into skills, examples, or answers.

## Derived Plugin Workflows

`x-router` is a routing skill.
`x-setup`, `x-review-pack`, `x-compound`, `x-memory-refresh`, and `x-handoff` are plugin workflows rather than claims of Musk-authored methods.
Safety boundaries, specialist-review rules, output templates, harness guidance, and behavior fixtures are also original project work.

## Claim Rule

When a source does not establish that Musk personally stated or endorsed a specific rule, use language such as `book-derived`, `associated with`, `inspired by`, or `the plugin maps this to`.
Do not turn a derived workflow into a direct quote or universal prescription.
